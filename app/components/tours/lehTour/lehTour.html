Day 1 :
Arrive at Leh. Transfer to Hotel to for rest. It is essential to spend the day resting so as to acclimatise adequately. In the evening, start out for some local sightseeing. Drive to the market, and walk to the LAMO house, a beautifully restored traditional house in old Leh. Continue on to the ruins of the Leh Palace, currently under the auspices of the Archeological Survey of India which is renovating this ancient castle overlooking Leh. End your day with a spectacular sunset from the Shanti Stupa, a white domed stupa overlooking the Leh valley. O/N at the hotel.
Day 2:
A full day visit to the monasteries of Alchi and Likir monasteries, driving through some beautiful terrain. The Alchi monastic complex (Alchi Choskor) comprises of 4 different settlements, of which the Alchi monastery is the oldest, being more than 900 years old- and has some fascinating ancient wall paintings not seen in any other monastery. The Likir monastery administers the Alchi monastery , and has more than 120 monks in attendance, along with an active school for young monks. You can also visit the ruins of the military fort of Basgo, as well as take in the beauty of the Indus and the Zanskar Rivers at their confluence. The Hall of Fame, Magnetic Field, and the Gurdwara Pather Sahib are other attractions on this route. An option is to first raft down from Nimoo to Sangam, and then complete your sightseeing. Return back to Leh. O/N at the hotel.

Day 3:
Take a ride up to Khardung La, the highest motorable road in the world. Drive downwards, towards Nubra Valley. Visit the Diskit monastery, from where you get a bird's eye view of the Shayok valley, and enjoy camel rides on the double humped camels at the Hunder sand dunes. O/N at the campt site.

Day 4:
Enjoy a leisurely breakfast, and drive back to Leh via the same route. You can opt to ride ATV bikes on the sand dunes at Khalsar. O/N at the hotel.
Day 5:
An early start today takes you through a fascinating journey over some breathtaking terrain and a high pass at Chang La, to visit the Pangong Lake. En route, visit the Thiksey monastery, Spend a few hours taking in the changing colours of the lake, and spend the night at in tents at the edge of the lake. O/N at the camp site.

Day 6:
Wake up to breakfast near the lake, and drive back to Leh. On the way, visit the Hemis monastery, the richest in the region. O/N at the hotel.
